Introduction to Python Course

This course is created and run by Aadit Ambadkar.


The Introduction to Python Course is a 5 session course (10-hour) program which exposes middle school students the basics of programming in Python, setting them up for success in a STEM-heavy future. Taught by me, a USACO Silver Competitor and 5-year python programmer, and custom crafted to teach Python to students from 5th to 8th grade.

Program Information and Dates

Dates and Times

The Intro to Python course will run for 5 days, from August 9th to August 13. It will be held online at 10:00am to 12:00pm PST (2 hours for 5 days). The camp is free, targeted towards rising 5th to 8th graders*. Spots are limited to the first 10 students to sign up**. Once again:

Camp Dates: August 9th to 13th

Camp Time: 10 am to 12 pm (PST)

Camp Capacity: 10 students

Curriculumn

The course will cover the following topics:

  1. Installing Python and Pycharm
  2. Datatypes (basics)
  3. Variables
  4. Data Structures (basics)
  5. Conditionals
  6. Loops
  7. Functions
  8. Recursion

Grading

Students will be expected to work on one continuous Final Project using what they learned each day on their own as homework. They will be required to send be their code via email (perhaps using your email) before midnight each day except for the final day. I will be giving them some guidelines of what should be added to their Final Project that day, and will be assessing their completion of the project through grades.

Grading System (per Day):

3 - Homework is completed and shows effort

2 - Homework is completed but lacks effort or is not turned in on time.

1 - Homework is partially completed

0 - Homework is not completed


Students are expected to spend about 30 minutes to 1 hour on the project each day. On the last day of the course, they will get about 30 minutes to 1 hour to ask any questions, and then they will showcase their final projects.

As a reminder, students can always send me an email asking questions or requesting due date extensions. This isn't really meant to be a school, and I want to maintain a positive environment.

*Any grade student can join. The student must be younger than 18 years old.

**Priority will be given to rising 5th to 8th graders. Spots will be given to students out of age range if no one else signed up for the course. Aside from grade, spots are distributed in a first come first serve fashion.
